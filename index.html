"Physics is about how things change"

The Universal Pattern

Every fundamental law in physics can be expressed as a relationship between quantities and their rates of change. This isn't a coincidence‚Äîit reflects something deep about the nature of physical law: physics describes dynamics, not statics.

Why Differential Equations?

Physical laws are local in space and time. They tell you what happens at a point, given the conditions at that point. To know what happens next, you need to know how fast things are changing‚Äîthat's a derivative. To predict the future or reconstruct the past, you integrate these local rules‚Äîthat's solving a differential equation.

This is why Newton's laws, Maxwell's equations, the Schr√∂dinger equation, Einstein's field equations, and essentially every fundamental physical law takes the form of a differential equation: they describe rates of change, not final states.

In this exploration, we'll see how velocity, acceleration, current, power, EMF, entropy flow, and force all share the same mathematical structure: they're all derivatives‚Äîrates at which one quantity changes with respect to another.

Why Stop at Acceleration?

Newton's second law relates force to acceleration: F = ma = m·∫ç. This is a second-order differential equation‚Äîit involves the second derivative of position. To solve it, you need two initial conditions: position x(0) and velocity v(0). That's why these are the "natural" variables in mechanics.

Jerk matters for engineering (smooth motor control, passenger comfort) but doesn't appear in fundamental laws. Most of physics stops at second derivatives!

Why This Matters

Understanding physics means understanding differential equations. When you see:

The deepest insight: Physical laws are local and causal. They tell you how things change in the next infinitesimal instant based on current conditions. That's why they're differential equations. To know what happens later, you integrate‚Äîyou add up all the infinitesimal changes. Physics is the study of change, and calculus is its natural language.
